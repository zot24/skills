# X Engagement Playbook

Expert at building authority and engagement on X (Twitter) through distribution engineering, strategic hooks, and conversation hijacking.

## Overview

- **Distribution engineering** over random tweeting - treat reach as a system
- **Hook-first writing** - first line decides everything
- **Conversation hijacking** - borrow attention from active threads
- **Practical signals** - real actions build credibility over theory
- **Content clusters** - interconnected posts that drive cross-traffic
- **Strategic timing** - post first, then reply to relevant threads

## Core Principles

### 1. Post First, Reply Second
Always post your content to your profile first, then use replies in relevant threads to pull traffic back to your tweet. Never let your best content die inside someone else's thread.

### 2. Hook → Context → Story → Lesson
Structure every post with a strong opening line. Bad: "I updated my Spanish DNI recently because..." Good: "People obsess over the '183-day rule'. But that's rarely the real issue."

### 3. Actions Over Opinions
Convert theory into practice. Show real examples, administrative steps, and concrete actions. People trust demonstrated experience over abstract advice.

## Quick Workflow

1. **Draft content** with a strong hook (controversial truth or myth-busting)
2. **Post to your profile** first
3. **Wait 10-30 minutes** for algorithm testing
4. **Find active threads** (20-200 likes, your topic) and add value
5. **Reply to comments** (not just main tweets) for lower competition
6. **Never drop naked links** - wrap in insight + context

## Content Formula

```
controversial truth + practical knowledge + real-life examples + conversation hijacking = reach
```

## Anti-Patterns

- Dropping naked links (looks spammy)
- Replying before posting your own content
- Leading with theory instead of examples
- Posting isolated tweets instead of topic clusters
- Replying only to main tweets (high competition)
